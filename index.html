Research Manuscripts

Predictive Processing and Dopaminergic Precision in Psychosis

Abstract

Schizophrenia spectrum disorders can be conceptualized as disturbances in hierarchical inference. Within predictive coding frameworks, dopamine modulates precision weighting of prediction errors. Elevated striatal dopamine synthesis capacity may amplify irrelevant stimuli, contributing to aberrant salience and delusion formation.

Introduction

The predictive processing framework models the brain as a Bayesian inference machine. Psychosis may arise when precision weighting becomes dysregulated.

Discussion

Computational psychiatry provides mechanistic explanation linking dopamine dysregulation to belief instability.

Neural Mechanisms of Sexual Reward and Vulnerability

Abstract

Sexual reward engages mesolimbic dopamine circuitry overlapping with systems implicated in depression and schizophrenia.

Discussion

Altered reward anticipation may serve as marker of motivational vulnerability.
