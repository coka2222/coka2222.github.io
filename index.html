About

I am currently completing my MSc in Neuropsychology at Maastricht University, where I conduct research within the Department of Cognitive Neuroscience. My academic focus centers on brain-behavior relationships, neuroimaging methodology, and the neural mechanisms underlying affect regulation and vulnerability to psychopathology.

My work engages with functional neuroimaging techniques, including functional near-infrared spectroscopy, and integrates theoretical models of reward processing, predictive inference, and executive control. I am particularly interested in how computational and neural frameworks can inform our understanding of maladaptive motivation and clinical risk.

Research Focus

Neuroimaging Methodology

Application of non-invasive neuroimaging techniques to investigate cortical activation patterns associated with cognitive and affective processes. Emphasis on signal processing, experimental design, and translational interpretation.

Reward Processing and Motivation

Investigation of dopaminergic circuitry and motivational salience in shaping adaptive and maladaptive behavior. Interest in predictive models of desire and reinforcement learning frameworks.

Psychopathology and Cognitive Vulnerability

Examination of neural and computational mechanisms contributing to compulsivity, affective dysregulation, and psychosis spectrum vulnerability.

Selected Writing

The Neural Architecture of Desire and Regulation

An integrative theoretical analysis of reward circuitry, predictive processing, and regulatory control in vulnerability to psychopathology.

Convergence of Neurobiology and Psychoanalytic Theory

Bachelor’s thesis examining how contemporary neuroscience findings intersect with classical models of unconscious processes and repression.
