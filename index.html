Neuroscience of Salience, Reward, and Self Regulation

Research portfolio exploring predictive processing, dopaminergic precision, sexual reward systems, psychosis vulnerability, and cortical self regulation.

Research Identity

My work investigates how hierarchical neural systems generate internal models of the environment, and how disruptions in precision-weighted inference contribute to psychopathology. I focus on dopaminergic salience attribution, reward processing, neurofeedback-based self regulation, and vulnerability mechanisms in schizophrenia spectrum disorders.

The overarching aim is mechanistic explanation. Rather than describing symptoms, I examine how neural computation, reinforcement learning, and cortical network dynamics give rise to psychiatric phenomena.

Research Articles

Computational Psychiatry

Predictive Processing, Dopaminergic Precision, and Aberrant Salience in Psychosis

Abstract

Schizophrenia spectrum disorders can be conceptualized as disorders of hierarchical inference. Dopaminergic dysregulation may distort precision weighting of prediction errors, producing pathological salience attribution and unstable belief updating. This article integrates computational models, neuroimaging findings, and pharmacological evidence to examine mechanistic pathways to psychosis.

The paper synthesizes evidence from PET studies demonstrating elevated striatal dopamine synthesis capacity, functional connectivity alterations in cortical hierarchies, and behavioral findings on probabilistic reasoning deficits. A predictive coding framework provides a unified explanation linking neurobiology and phenomenology.

Reward Systems and Sexuality

Neural Mechanisms of Sexual Reward Processing and Vulnerability to Psychopathology

Abstract

Sexual reward processing is mediated by mesolimbic dopaminergic circuitry that overlaps with neural systems implicated in mood disorders and schizophrenia. Alterations in reward sensitivity may contribute to anhedonia, compulsive behavior, and motivational deficits.

The article reviews neuroimaging evidence demonstrating ventral striatal activation during sexual cue exposure, clinical findings on sexual dysfunction in psychiatric populations, and reinforcement learning accounts of motivational vulnerability. Sexual reward processing is proposed as an index of broader motivational system integrity.

Neuroimaging and Self Regulation

Neurofeedback as a Modulator of Cortical Self Regulation

Abstract

Neurofeedback enables individuals to modulate neural activity through real time feedback. Grounded in operant conditioning and reinforcement learning, neurofeedback may induce plastic changes within cortical regulatory networks.

This paper reviews EEG, fMRI, and fNIRS evidence, examining whether neurofeedback produces region specific modulation or strengthens distributed executive control systems. Methodological challenges and placebo effects are discussed.
